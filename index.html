House retention
Same partner earns both pools
If one partner both originated and executed, they earn both pools combined. House retention still applies. No forced split.
Joint origination
Two partners who jointly introduced a client split the origination pool equally. Must be agreed and logged before Iroko is notified. Register date is the record.
Introduction register governs
Date of first logged introduction determines origination ownership. Disputes resolved by register date only — not by recollection. Maintained by P1, accessible to all.
Strip income is house income first
All margin strip flows into the company. Personal distribution only via dividend on a majority-agreed schedule — not treated as commission.
New person involvement
Their share comes from the relevant pool — it does not increase the pool size. Must be pre-agreed in writing per deal before work begins.
Annual review
Pool percentages reviewed annually by unanimous vote. Changes require 6 months notice and apply only to deals originated after the change date.
Shotgun clause
Any partner can name a price at which they will buy all other shares OR sell all their own. Receiving partners have 30 days to elect. Forces a fair price — initiator does not know if they will be buyer or seller.
Forced exit grounds
Criminal conviction, material breach, insolvency, misrepresentation at founding. Performance grounds require formal warning, 90-day remediation and 2/3 vote — not unilateral action.

The four tests before any equity offer
Replacement test
Could you hire someone on a salary to do what this person does? If yes, hire them. Partners are for people whose value lies in who they are and who they know.
Multiplier test
Does this person multiply revenue potential — new service line, new market, new client segment — or just add capacity? Multipliers earn equity. Capacity additions earn salaries.
Alignment test
Are they willing to commit fully — no day job, no competing obligations — and take the same personal financial risk? Full commitment is a prerequisite for equity.
Unanimous vote test
All three founding partners must agree. One strong objection is a signal to wait, even if the other two are enthusiastic.
The most common early mistake is giving equity to people who should have been employees or commission introducers. Equity is permanent and dilutive. Be conservative — it is always easier to give more equity later.
Iroko working capital model
Milestone-based advance with clawback on first fee and strip income
Maximum total Iroko exposure: R300,000 across all tranches — manageable for a firm with $4bn in executed transactions.
Tranche 1 — On signingR75,000
Paid on execution of the signed introducer agreement. Covers immediate operating costs — legal fees, compliance setup, first month expenses. Iroko risk at this point: R75k.
Tranche 2 — On 3 formal introductionsR75,000
Paid when AlphaGen delivers 3 formally documented investor introductions — written notification to Iroko with institution name, contact and confirmed meeting date. Iroko cumulative risk: R150k.
Tranche 3 — On first NDA or term sheetR75,000
Paid when at least one introduced investor has signed an NDA or entered term sheet negotiations. Confirms genuine investor engagement. Iroko cumulative risk: R225k.
Tranche 4 — Monthly sustaining (optional)R25,000/month
Only if first close has not occurred by month 4. Capped at 3 months (R75k total). Triggers a formal review at month 6. Maximum additional Iroko risk: R75k.
The advance is recouped from the first fee — Iroko nets it off. AlphaGen never writes a cheque back. The premium is linked to close timing: faster close, lower cost.

Under Option D (hybrid), the advance recoupment is split: 50% from the upfront fee on close, 50% from the first 12 months of strip income deducted at source by Iroko.
Fee component — 50% of advance
Recouped from the upfront fee on first close using the timing-based premium schedule. The lower upfront fee under Option D reflects this — AlphaGen accepts a smaller immediate payment in exchange for the strip.
Strip component — 50% of advance
Remaining 50% recouped from the first 12 months of strip income, deducted at source before passing payments to AlphaGen. No timing premium — Iroko earns margin during this period anyway.
On a $50m deal at 30bp: AlphaGen earns $150k/year in strip. The first ~$15k (50% of R300k at current rates) is withheld in year one. By month 13 the advance is fully recouped and AlphaGen receives 100% of strip payments for the remaining asset life.

Iroko's protection
Advance repayable by the company — not personally by partners. If no deals close within 18 months (longstop date), advance converts to recoverable company debt. No personal liability unless fraud is committed.
AlphaGen's protection
Advance not repayable if failure to close was caused by Iroko — withdrawing a transaction, changing deal terms materially, or failing to progress an introduced investor in good faith within 60 days. Force majeure carve-out for market events.
Pipeline transfer clause
If partnership dissolves while deals are in progress, cooperation required for 30–60 day handover. Originating partner retains right to tail fee — 50% if deal closes within 12 months of exit, 25% in months 12–24.
Named pipeline protection
Any SA investor formally registered with Iroko at signing is exclusively AlphaGen's for the tail period. Iroko's own team cannot approach them directly. This is non-negotiable.
